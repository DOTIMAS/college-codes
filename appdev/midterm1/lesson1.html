The Prehistoric Era (3000 BCE)

The prehistoric era, also known as prehistory, refers to the period of human history before the invention of writing systems, which occurred around 3000 BCE. This era is divided into three main periods: the Paleolithic (Old Stone Age), Mesolithic (Middle Stone Age), and Neolithic (New Stone Age). During the Paleolithic period, early humans were primarily hunter-gatherers who relied on stone tools for survival. They lived in small nomadic groups, used fire, and developed basic forms of communication through gestures and cave art. The Mesolithic period marked a gradual shift toward more refined tools and the beginnings of permanent settlements, as people began to experiment with agriculture and animal domestication.

The Neolithic period brought about a significant transformation in human societies, as agriculture became more established and people began to form larger, more complex communities. This period saw the rise of farming, animal husbandry, and the construction of permanent dwellings. Technological advances, such as polished stone tools, pottery, and weaving, contributed to more stable and organized societies.
